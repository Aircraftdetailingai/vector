Free Aircraft Detailing Software: What You Get and When to Upgrade

If you're looking for free aircraft detailing software, I have good news and a reality check. The good news: free tiers exist and they're genuinely useful for getting started. The reality check: if you're detailing more than a handful of aircraft per month, you'll need the paid features eventually. Here's how to think about it.

What Free Gets You

Most free CRM plans in the aircraft detailing space give you the basics:

  • A limited number of quotes per month (typically 3-5)
  • Access to the aircraft database with labor hours for 300+ models
  • FAA tail number autofill — enter N12345 and the system pulls the aircraft info
  • Professional quote pages that customers can view and accept online
  • Basic customer management — save customer info and quote history
  • Directory listing — appear in aviation detailer directories so customers can find you

This is enough to test the software, build your first few quotes, and see if the workflow fits your business. It's also enough for a detailer who's just starting out and doing a few jobs per month.

What Free Doesn't Get You

The features that actually drive business growth are behind the paid plans. Here's what you're missing on free:

Online Payments

On most free plans, customers can view your quote but can't pay through it. You're still collecting payment separately — via Zelle, check, wire transfer, or a separate invoicing tool. This adds friction, delays payment, and looks less professional than a one-click checkout on the quote page.

Unlimited Quotes

If you're doing 10-20 jobs per month, 5 quotes doesn't cut it. You need to quote more than you close, and you need to quote fast. The paid plans remove the cap entirely.

Recurring Service Tracking

This is the feature that turns one-time customers into recurring revenue. Setting intervals per aircraft (wax every 90 days, maintenance wash monthly) and getting automatic reminders when service is due. On free, you're tracking this manually — which means you're not tracking it at all.

Google Calendar Integration

Automatic scheduling based on your actual availability. The paid plans check your Google Calendar and suggest the next open slot that fits the job duration. On free, you're flipping between tabs trying to find an open date.

Customer Portal

The paid plans give your customers a branded portal where they can see their job history, before/after photos, upcoming service dates, and rebook with one tap. On free, the customer relationship lives in email and text messages.

Automated Review Requests

Seven days after a job completes, the paid plans automatically send the customer a review request. Reviews are the single best marketing tool for a detailing business. On free, you're remembering to ask — or more likely, forgetting.

When to Upgrade

Here's my honest framework:

Stay on free if:

  • You're doing fewer than 5 jobs per month
  • You're testing whether aircraft detailing is a viable business
  • You don't need online payment collection yet
  • You're a solo operator who can manage scheduling manually

Upgrade to Pro ($79/mo) when:

  • You're consistently quoting more than 5 jobs per month
  • You want customers to pay online through the quote
  • You want automated follow-ups and review requests
  • You're ready to look and operate like a professional business

Upgrade to Business ($149/mo) when:

  • You have a team (even just one helper)
  • You need to assign and track jobs across multiple people
  • You want proactive recurring service reminders sent to customers
  • You're managing more than 20 recurring customer relationships

The Math

Here's the calculation that made the decision easy for me. A Pro plan costs $79/month. One quote built in 3 minutes instead of 45 saves me roughly 40 minutes. At my billing rate, those 40 minutes are worth about $130. The software pays for itself on the first quote of the month.

If the automated review requests generate even one new customer per quarter (and they will — reviews are the highest-converting marketing for local service businesses), the ROI is easily 10-20x the subscription cost.
