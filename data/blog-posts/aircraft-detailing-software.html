Why Aircraft Detailers Need Purpose-Built Software in 2026

I've been detailing aircraft for 15 years. For most of that time, I ran my business on a combination of spreadsheets, text messages, and handwritten notes on yellow legal pads. Sound familiar?

Here's the thing — it worked. Until it didn't. The day I showed up to detail a G550 and realized I'd quoted it using Challenger 350 hours was the day I knew I needed something better. That mistake cost me two days of unpaid labor and a very uncomfortable conversation with the aircraft owner.

The Problem With General-Purpose Tools

Most detailers I know have tried some combination of QuickBooks for invoicing, Google Calendar for scheduling, and maybe a generic CRM like HubSpot or Jobber. None of these tools understand our industry. They don't know that a Gulfstream G4 takes 72 hours to polish. They don't know that brightwork on a Citation is a completely different job than brightwork on a Global 7500. They can't calculate your quote based on actual aircraft surface area.

Aircraft detailing isn't automotive detailing with bigger vehicles. The pricing model is fundamentally different. We price by labor hours, not by vehicle size category. Every aircraft has different surface areas, different materials, different access requirements. A tool built for car detailers will never handle this correctly.

What Aircraft Detailing Software Actually Needs

After years of frustration, I started building what I wished existed. The requirements were simple:

  • An aircraft database with real labor hours. Not estimates — actual hours based on surface area calculations for 300+ aircraft models. Select the aircraft, check the services, and the quote calculates itself.
  • FAA tail number lookup. Customer gives you a tail number, the system pulls the make, model, and year automatically. No more asking "what kind of plane is it?"
  • Professional customer-facing quotes. Not a PDF attachment — a live link where the customer can view the quote, accept it, and pay online. On their phone, at the FBO, right now.
  • Recurring service tracking. Wax every 90 days. Ceramic inspection every 6 months. De-ice boots annually. The system tracks intervals per aircraft and tells you when service is due — before the customer has to ask.
  • Before and after photo documentation. Every job should have a visual record. For the customer, for your portfolio, and for warranty documentation on ceramic coatings.

The Real ROI of Dedicated Software

Let me give you real numbers from my own operation. Before I had purpose-built software, I was spending roughly 45 minutes per quote — looking up aircraft specs, calculating hours, building a PDF, emailing it, following up. Now it takes under 3 minutes. Select the aircraft, check the services, send.

At 20 quotes per month, that's 14 hours saved. At my billing rate, that's over $2,600 in recovered time every single month. The software pays for itself before the end of the first week.

But the bigger win is accuracy. When your hours are pulled from a verified database instead of your memory, your quotes are right the first time. No more underquoting a job and eating the difference. No more overquoting and losing the work to a competitor.

What About the Customer Experience?

Here's something most detailers don't think about — the customer experience of receiving a quote. When you send a PDF attachment via email, the customer has to download it, open it, maybe squint at it on their phone. Then they have to reply to accept, and you have to send them a separate payment link.

Compare that to a branded quote page with a live link. The customer taps it, sees the services, sees the price, accepts, and pays — all in one flow. On their phone, in 30 seconds. The acceptance rate difference is significant. I've seen it go from roughly 60% to over 80% just by removing friction from the acceptance process.

Recurring Revenue Changes Everything

The detailers who are building real businesses — not just trading hours for dollars — are the ones who've figured out recurring services. When you track service intervals per aircraft and proactively reach out when wax is due or when it's time for a ceramic inspection, you stop being a vendor and start being a trusted maintenance partner.

Purpose-built aircraft detailing software makes this automatic. Set the interval, and the system reminds you (and optionally the customer) when it's time. No more hoping they remember to call you back.
